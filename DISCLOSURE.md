# Coordinated Disclosure and Contact Policy

## Contact

For security reports, licensing questions, partnership requests, or private disclosure coordination, use:

- https://wpservice.pro/contact/

Do not publish vulnerability details, exploit code, customer data, private keys, tokens, or operational infrastructure details in public issues, discussions, pull requests, or social posts before coordination.

## Good-Faith Research

Good-faith security research should avoid service disruption, data destruction, privacy violations, persistence, social engineering, phishing, physical attacks, spam, denial-of-service testing, and testing against third-party systems without permission.

If you encounter sensitive data during research, stop testing, do not save or share the data, and report the exposure privately.

## Disclosure Process

Reports should include enough detail to reproduce and evaluate the issue. We may ask follow-up questions, request a reasonable embargo while a fix is prepared, and credit the reporter when appropriate and requested.

Public disclosure should happen only after a fix or mitigation is available, or after we mutually agree on a disclosure timeline.

## Commercial Contact

Commercial use, resale, white-label arrangements, managed-service use, or access to private SaaS components requires a separate written agreement.
